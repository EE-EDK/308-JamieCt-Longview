## 1. PREMISES

**A.** The Landlords agree to rent to the Tenants the municipality located at **308 Jamie Ct, Longview, TX 75605** (the "Premises") for primary use as a residential premise. The Premise is more particularly described as follows: the entire property contained within the property deed and all items located on the property.

**B.** Subject to the provisions of this Lease, apart from the Tenants and their dependents, no other persons will live in the Premises without the prior consent of the Landlords through some medium of notice, nor will the property be subleased without consent of the Landlord in the same manner. As of the effective date of this Lease, the Tenants' dependent minor **Kenlea K. Geers** is an authorized occupant of the Premises. She is not a party to this Lease and accepts no liability, contract, or expectation under it beyond the use of the home. Jared and Kayla Geers take full responsibility for all matters pertaining to the expectations of this Lease wherever Kenlea is concerned.

**C.** No guest of the Tenants may occupy the Premises without the prior consent of the Landlord if that guest is staying for more than two weeks.

**D.** The Tenants are authorized to keep two (2) large-breed German Shepherd canines on the Premises under the following terms. The Tenants take full legal responsibility for both dogs on and off the rented property. A pet fee of **$20.00 per dog per month** ($40.00 total) is added to the base rent each month, bringing the total monthly rent obligation to **$1,920.00**. Should either or both dogs cause damage in excess of $1,000.00, the Tenants will either rectify the damage within two (2) months or pay for half of the amount exceeding the deposit as compensation. No additional pets or animals may be kept in or about the Premises without prior written consent of the Landlord. Any such consent shall be given in the form of a written addendum to this Lease.

**E.** There will be no indoor smoking on the premises. If the property becomes such that it requires professional cleaning to remove the odor of smoke inside the home, the Tenants will agree to absorb the cost to clean the property to a reasonable level of neutral odor. Any costs associated with smoking are unrelated to the rental deposit and shall be paid separately from the rental deposit.

## 2. TERM

The **4th** term of this Lease shall start on **May 1st, 2026**. The **4th** term will cycle on a monthly basis for one year from the start date, at which point a **fifth** term can be agreed upon. The cycle is stated to give preference to the Tenants for a move-out date at any point within the year term, with the only stipulation being a notice given to the Landlords 30 days in advance of movement in order to secure a new tenant. This does not include life-altering emergencies, where it is impossible to manage the 30-day window. Such emergencies include for example: death of a tenant, sudden job loss without notice requiring a quick move, or grave illness. If the tenant moves within the last month of payment beyond the 1st of the month, they agree to pay a percentage of the rental deposit against the monthly rent. For example, if the tenant moves out May 15, then the tenant's rental deposit will subtract half the worth of the month's rent.

## 3. RENT

Rent shall be paid in full at **$1,880.00** (base) within the first week of each month. Including the pet fee described in Section 1D, the total monthly payment due is **$1,920.00**. This amount covers only the lease of the Premises and does not include any other fees incurred by living at the property such as utility services. Such fees and services will be selected and maintained by the Tenants. Rent will be paid using any method available on the Avail application, which is the system used for managing the property. No method shall be chosen that, once posted, requires more than a week after the last day of the first week of each month to post. To summarize, all payments must be in the account of the Landlords within two weeks of the first of the month, barring extenuating circumstances agreed upon *in writing* by Tenants and Landlords. Failure to pay rent within one week of the posted date shall result in a fee of **$175.00** as allowed under Texas law for each two weeks not paid from the first day late. The first late payment will be a warning, and Tenant will be informed of lateness with a 5-day grace period to post payment.

### Basis for the 2025–2026 Rent Increase

In the interest of full transparency, the Landlords provide the following accounting of how the rent adjustment from the 3rd term ($1,825.00) to the 4th term ($1,880.00) was determined.

The Landlords' primary carrying cost on this property is the monthly mortgage payment, which as of April 2026 is structured as follows: $323.99 in principal, $310.16 in interest, and **$512.93 in escrow** covering property taxes and homeowner's insurance. It is this escrow component — the portion most directly subject to market forces — that governs annual rent adjustments under the terms of this Lease.

To quantify the appropriate adjustment, the Landlords referenced two inflation benchmarks published by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS):

- **Full-year 2025 CPI-U (December 2024 to December 2025): 2.7%**, representing the broadest measure of consumer price change for the calendar year.
- **Shelter-specific CPI (year-over-year, February 2026): 3.0%**, representing price changes within the housing category — the measure most directly aligned with property tax, insurance, and housing-related operating costs.

The Landlords applied the **3.0% shelter CPI** figure as the basis for this adjustment. This rate was chosen over the broader headline CPI because it more accurately reflects the cost environment affecting property ownership and carrying costs. The March 2026 headline CPI of 3.3% was reviewed but was not used; that figure reflects a transient spike in energy prices (gasoline +18.9%, fuel oil +44.2%) and does not represent sustained housing cost pressure. The 3.0% rate is also well within the 5% annual cap established in this Lease.

The resulting adjustment is as follows:

| Item | Previous (3rd Term) | Current (4th Term) |
|---|---|---|
| Base rent | $1,825.00 | $1,880.00 |
| Pet fee (2 dogs × $20) | $40.00 | $40.00 |
| **Total monthly due** | **$1,865.00** | **$1,920.00** |
| Increase (base only) | — | +$55.00 (+3.0%) |

Future changes to the rent amount will — as of this Lease — continue to be based on increases in the Landlord's property tax and homeowner's insurance, referenced against the shelter-specific CPI or the full-year CPI-U, whichever is more directly applicable. Should the Landlord's loan be paid off, the yearly increase will be based on a 5-year average percentage of yearly inflation, not to exceed 5%. The basis and calculation for any such change will be documented in the rent section of each renewal term. Such changes in cost will occur each year between April and May.

## 4. SECURITY DEPOSIT [STRENGTHENED]

Upon execution of this Lease, Tenants **have already paid the required $1,500.00** as security for the performance by Tenants of the terms of this Lease. The deposit will be returned to Tenants, without interest, following the full and faithful performance by Tenants of this Lease, subject to the following:

**A.** In the event of damage to the House caused by Tenants or Tenants' family, agents or visitors, Landlords may use funds from the deposit to repair. If a repair requirement is caused by Tenants due to negligence that exceeds $1,500.00 and the item is not covered by either home or rental insurance, then the Tenant absorbs the liability of rendering the damage to a previous state within 3 months. Failure to do so will result in a 2-week notice of eviction followed by a filing for relevant legal claims.

**B.** Within **30 days** after Tenants vacate the Premises, Landlords shall provide Tenants with a written itemized statement of any deductions made from the security deposit, along with any remaining balance owed to Tenants. This statement shall be mailed or delivered to Tenants' forwarding address.

**C.** Tenants must provide Landlords with a written forwarding address at or before the time of vacating the Premises. Failure to provide a forwarding address does not relieve Tenants of responsibility to receive the deposit accounting; Landlords shall send to the last known address on file.

**D.** In the event the Premises are sold or title is transferred during the Lease term, Landlords shall either transfer the security deposit to the new owner and provide written notice of such transfer to Tenants, or return the deposit to Tenants prior to the transfer.

## 5. ASSIGNMENT AND SUBLETTING

**A.** Tenants shall not assign this Lease or sublet or grant any concession or license to use the House or any part of the Premises without Landlords' prior written consent.

**B.** Any assignment, subletting, concession, or license without the prior written consent of Landlords, or an assignment or subletting by operation of law, shall be void and, at Landlords' option, terminate this Lease. The Tenants will also absorb all liability from damages caused by individuals who were assigned, sublet, conceded, or licensed without approval.

**C.** If the Tenants decide to allow another family member or members on the premises for the term of this Lease, up to two family members may stay in the house for a period of one month without required consent of the Landlords.

## 6. ALTERATIONS AND IMPROVEMENTS

**A.** Tenants shall make no alterations to the House or construct any building or make other improvements without the prior written consent of Landlord. This does not include items that are easily changed such as pins to hang pictures, towel racks, shower head adjustments, or other items that can be reasonably reversed or altered by one person within two days. Changes that require more than two days shall be billed to the Tenant.

**B.** All permanent alterations, changes, and improvements built, constructed, or placed on or around the House by Tenants, with the exception of fixtures properly removable without damage to the House and movable personal property, shall, unless otherwise provided by written agreement between Landlords and Tenants, be the property of the Landlords and remain at the expiration or earlier termination of this Lease. Tenants shall not alter landscaping, trees, exterior structures, or fencing without prior written consent of the Landlords.

**C.** Items agreed upon as a partnership, such as paid work done by the Tenants, or repairs that alter the basic state of the house will be agreed upon in writing. The receipts and tracking of payments or costs will be tracked in the Avail application for transparency and tax purposes.

**D.** Tenants shall not install any new appliances on the Premises without prior written consent of the Landlords. Any appliance installed with consent that was not part of the original Premises must be removed by Tenants at Lease expiration unless Landlords agree in writing to retain it.

## 7. DAMAGE TO PREMISES

If the House, or any part of the House, shall be partially damaged by fire or other casualty not due to Tenants' negligence or willful act, or that of Tenants' family, agent, or visitor, there shall be an abatement of rent corresponding with the time during which, and the extent to which, the House is untenantable. If Landlords shall decide not to rebuild or repair, the term of this Lease shall end, and the rent shall be prorated up to the time of the damage.

## 8. UTILITIES

As mentioned in the section governing rent, all utilities will be covered by the Tenants. Tenants must maintain at the bare minimum a means of trash removal and electricity. This is to ensure that the property is not damaged by infestation or mold due to improper waste removal or a temperature that is left above 82 degrees Fahrenheit in combination with a humidity above 60%. The house will be maintained both above 32 degrees Fahrenheit and below 82 degrees Fahrenheit. If mold accumulates due to improper management of temperature or humidity, the Tenants will be liable for mold remediation.

## 9. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIR

**A.** Tenants will keep and maintain the Premises and appliances in good and sanitary condition during the term of this Lease. In particular, Tenants shall, at Tenants' sole expense, make all required repairs to the plumbing, range, oven heating apparatus, electric and gas fixtures, other mechanical devices and systems, floors, lawn, decorative foliage, ceilings and walls whenever damage to such items shall have resulted from Tenant's misuse, waste, neglect, or that of the Tenants' family, agent, or visitor.

**B.** Tenants agree that no signs shall be placed, or painting or remodeling done on or about the House by Tenants without the prior written consent of Landlord.

**C.** Tenants agree to promptly notify Landlords in the event of any damage, defect, or destruction of the House, or the failure of any of Landlords' appliances or mechanical systems. Except for repairs or replacements that are the obligation of Tenant pursuant to Subsection A above, Landlord shall use best efforts to repair or replace such damaged or defective area, appliance, or mechanical system in compliance with Texas Property Code §92.056 and §92.0561. Tenants acknowledge their statutory rights under those provisions and agree to provide written notice to Landlords through the Avail system before exercising any statutory remedy.

## 10. RENTAL INSURANCE [STRENGTHENED]

Tenants shall maintain a renters insurance policy throughout the entire term of this Lease. The policy must provide coverage for Tenants' personal property and personal liability in amounts reasonable for the household. Tenants must furnish Landlords with written proof of a current policy within **30 days** of executing this Lease and within 30 days of any renewal, lapse, or change in coverage. Landlords may request updated proof of insurance at any time. Failure to obtain or maintain renters insurance, or failure to provide proof upon request, shall constitute a breach of this Lease. Tenants' insurance obligations are independent of and do not limit Landlords' own property insurance coverage.

## 11. RIGHT OF INSPECTION

Landlords and Landlords' agents shall have the right at all reasonable times during the term of this Lease and any renewal of this Lease to enter the house for the purpose of inspecting the Premises and/or making any repairs to the Premises or other item as required under this Lease. Such inspection of the house shall require notification to the Tenants within 24 hours unless there is reasonable suspicion under Texas law that requires prudent examination of the Premise for items of an illegal or maliciously suspicious nature. General inspection around the property that does not cross the fence line or involve home entry will not require notification. If less than 24 hours is given for a home entry and no items of note are obtained, the Landlords will remove **$300.00** from the bill of the month of such inspection in good faith. Regardless of the reason for inspection, a one-hour confirmation must be submitted to the Tenants before home entry, and only the Landlords listed on this Lease can perform an inspection less than 24 hours in nature. If no confirmation is obtained within 1 hour of a less-than-24-hours inspection, then police officials will be contacted, and the issue will become that of a criminal or civil nature in terms of approach and entry to the Premises.

## 12. DISPLAY OF SIGNS

During the 30-day window of a written relinquishment of Tenancy, or within 30 days of the one-year mark of this Lease, "For Sale," "For Rent," "Vacancy," or similar signs may be placed by Landlords on or about the House, and entry to show the House to prospective purchasers or tenants may be accomplished by Landlords with proper notification to current Tenants.

## 13. HOLDOVER BY TENANT [STRENGTHENED]

Should Tenants remain in possession of the House with the **written** consent of Landlords after the expiration of the Term of this Lease, a new tenancy shall be created which shall be subject to all the terms and conditions of this Lease, plus any additional terms agreed upon at such time. If Tenant holds over **without Landlords' written consent**, Landlord is entitled to a penalty of **two times (2×) the regular monthly rent**, pro-rated on a daily basis for each day Tenant remains in possession beyond the Lease expiration date, in addition to any other remedies available to Landlords under Texas law.

---

## 14. SURRENDER OF PREMISES

At the expiration of the Lease, Tenant shall quit and surrender the House in as good a condition as it was at the commencement of this Lease, reasonable wear and tear and damages by the elements excepted. Examples of reasonable wear include fading of the carpet, minor scrapes to paint no more than one inch that can be repaired with paint, broken blinds from age and sun damage, death of plants on Premises, and broken down insulation in the attic.

## 15. FORFEITURE OF SECURITY DEPOSIT — DEFAULT

It is understood and agreed that Tenants shall not attempt to apply or deduct any portion of any security deposit from the last or any month's rent, or use or apply any such security deposit at any time in lieu of payment of rent. If Tenant fails to comply, such security deposit shall be forfeited, and Landlords may recover the rent due as if any such deposit had not been applied or deducted from the rent due. For the purposes of this paragraph, it shall be presumed that a Tenant leaving the Premises while owing rent is making an attempted deduction of deposits. Furthermore, any deposit shall be held as a guarantee that Tenants shall perform the obligations of the Lease and shall be forfeited by the Tenants should Tenants breach any of the terms and conditions of this Lease. In the event of default, by Tenants, of any obligation in this Lease which is not cured by Tenant within fifteen (15) days notice from Landlord, then in addition to forfeiture of the Security Deposit, Landlords may pursue any other remedy available at Texas and federal law, equity or otherwise. As an example, failure to pay a late rent penalty will result in a recorded removal from the security deposit along with communication of that amount to the Tenants.

## 16. ABANDONMENT [STRENGTHENED]

If at any time during the term of this Lease, Tenants abandon the House or any of Tenants' personal property in or about the House, Landlords shall have the following rights: Landlords may, at Landlords' option, enter the House by any means without liability to Tenant for damages and may relet the House for the whole or any part of the then unexpired term, and may receive and collect all rent payable by virtue of such reletting. At Landlord's option, Landlords may also hold Tenants liable for any difference between the rent that would have been payable under this Lease during the balance of the unexpired term and the net rent for such period realized by Landlords by means of such reletting. Before selling, disposing of, or destroying any personal property left by Tenants, Landlords shall provide **30 days' written notice** to Tenants at their last known address or forwarding address. Landlords are entitled to presume that Tenants have abandoned the House if Tenants remove substantially all of Tenants' furnishings and personal possessions from the House, if the House is suddenly unoccupied for a period of two (2) consecutive weeks outside of vacation or business-related travel, or if it would otherwise be reasonable for Landlords to presume under the circumstances that the Tenants have abandoned the House.

## 17. SECURITY [STRENGTHENED]

Tenant acknowledges that Landlord does not provide a security alarm system or any security for the House or for Tenants and that any such alarm system or security service, if provided, is not represented or warranted to be complete in all respects or to protect Tenants from all harm. Tenants hereby release Landlords from any loss, suit, claim, charge, damage, or injury resulting from lack of security or failure of security. This is mitigated by the requirement of rental insurance under §10. All security devices required by Texas law — including door locks, window latches, smoke detectors, and carbon monoxide detectors — shall be installed and maintained by the Landlords in compliance with Texas Property Code §92.153, §92.154, and §92.2611 (smoke and carbon monoxide alarms). Tenants shall promptly notify Landlords via the Avail system if any required safety device is inoperable, damaged, or missing. Tenants shall not disable, remove, or tamper with any required safety device.

Jared Geers and Kayla Geers are jointly and severally liable for all obligations arising under this Lease, including without limitation the payment of rent, late fees, damages, and any other amounts due. ## 22. MECHANIC'S LIENS [NEW]

Tenants shall not permit any mechanic's lien, materialman's lien, or other lien to be filed or to remain against the Premises or any improvement thereon by reason of any work, labor, services, or materials performed at the request of, or with the prior knowledge of, Tenants. If any such lien is filed, Tenants shall cause it to be released, discharged, or bonded over within **30 days** after receiving written notice of the filing, at Tenants' sole cost and expense. Failure to timely discharge any such lien shall constitute a material breach of this Lease. Landlords may, at their sole discretion, discharge any such lien on Tenants' behalf, and Tenants shall reimburse Landlords for all costs and reasonable attorney's fees incurred in doing so.

## 23. SUBORDINATION OF LEASE; ESTOPPEL [NEW]

This Lease is and shall remain subordinate to any existing or future mortgage, deed of trust, or other encumbrance placed on the Premises by Landlords, including the current loanDepot.com, LLC mortgage (Loan #5001979151). Tenants agree to attorn to any successor owner or mortgagee who acquires the Premises through foreclosure, sale, or other transfer, and to continue performing under this Lease as though no transfer had occurred. Upon written request by Landlords or any lender, Tenants shall execute and deliver within **10 days** an estoppel certificate or tenant recognition agreement certifying the status of this Lease, including: (a) the commencement and expiration dates; (b) the current monthly rent; (c) the amount of any security deposit held; (d) whether any defaults exist; and (e) such other reasonable information as may be requested. Tenants' failure to deliver such a certificate within the required time shall be deemed Tenants' confirmation that this Lease is in full force and effect, with no existing defaults, as of the date of the request.

## 24. SEVERABILITY [NEW]

If any provision of this Lease, or its application to any person or circumstance, is held invalid or unenforceable by a court of competent jurisdiction, the remainder of this Lease and the application of such provision to other persons or circumstances shall remain unaffected. Each remaining provision of this Lease shall be valid and enforceable to the fullest extent permitted by applicable Texas law. An invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid and enforceable, consistent with the parties' original intent.

## 25. LANDLORD'S CONTRACTUAL LIEN [NEW]

Pursuant to Texas Property Code Chapter 54, Landlords have a lien on all nonexempt personal property of Tenants that is located at the Premises, to secure the payment of rent that is due and unpaid. This lien is established by contract as permitted by Texas Property Code §54.021 and supplements any statutory lien rights available to Landlords. Landlords shall enforce this lien only in accordance with applicable Texas law, and shall follow all statutory procedures required for lien enforcement. Nothing in this section expands Landlords' rights beyond those permitted by Texas Property Code Chapter 54.

## 26. VACATION RENTAL AND SHORT-TERM RENTAL PROHIBITION [NEW]

Tenants shall not list, advertise, or use the Premises or any portion thereof as a short-term rental, vacation rental, transient lodging, or any accommodation for compensation — including but not limited to listings on Airbnb, VRBO, HomeAway, Booking.com, or any substantially similar platform, service, or application. The Premises are leased exclusively for private residential use by the named Tenants and the authorized occupants identified in §1B. Tenants may not collect rent, fees, or any compensation from any third party for use of or access to the Premises. Any violation of this section shall constitute a material breach of this Lease and shall entitle Landlords to immediate termination of the tenancy in addition to all other available remedies.

Landlords affirm compliance with the federal Fair Housing Act (42 U.S.C. §3601 et seq.) and the Texas Fair Housing Act (Texas Property Code Ch. 301). ## 28. ATTORNEY'S FEES [NEW]

In any legal action, arbitration, or proceeding arising out of or related to this Lease — including actions to collect unpaid rent, enforce Lease terms, recover damages, or obtain injunctive relief — the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and court costs from the non-prevailing party, to the extent authorized by Texas law including Texas Property Code §92.109 and applicable Texas Civil Practice and Remedies Code provisions. This right is in addition to any other remedies available under this Lease or applicable law.

## 29. LIMITATION OF LANDLORD'S LIABILITY [NEW]

Landlords shall not be liable to Tenants, Tenants' authorized occupants, guests, agents, or visitors for any personal injury, loss of or damage to personal property, or other loss caused by: (a) the acts or omissions of any third party; (b) theft, vandalism, fire, water intrusion, storm, or other casualty; (c) failure of utilities not controlled by Landlords; or (d) any condition of the Premises not attributable to Landlords' own negligence. Tenants are strongly encouraged to maintain renters insurance (§10) to protect against such risks. This limitation does not apply to losses caused by Landlords' gross negligence or willful misconduct.

## 30. CONDITION OF PREMISES; MOVE-IN CHECKLIST [NEW]

Prior to or within **72 hours** after Tenants take or resume possession under this Lease, Tenants shall inspect the Premises and document the condition of all rooms, appliances, fixtures, and surfaces on a move-in inspection checklist. Tenants shall return the completed checklist to Landlords, via the Avail system, within 72 hours of taking possession. Any damage or defect not documented on the move-in checklist shall be presumed to have occurred during Tenants' occupancy and may be charged against the security deposit at move-out. Tenants' failure to complete and return the checklist within the required time shall constitute Tenants' acknowledgment that the Premises were received in good and habitable condition. Landlords may conduct a move-out inspection at Lease expiration, with reasonable notice, to assess the condition of the Premises against the move-in checklist.

## 31. DISTURBANCES [NEW]

Tenants shall not engage in, permit, or encourage any activity on or about the Premises that constitutes a nuisance, disturbs the peace, or unreasonably interferes with the quiet enjoyment of neighboring properties. This includes but is not limited to: excessive noise at any hour; noise audible beyond the property boundaries between 10:00 p.m. and 8:00 a.m.; offensive odors; or any activity that violates applicable Gregg County or City of Longview noise or nuisance ordinances. Tenants are responsible for the conduct of their authorized occupants, guests, and pets. After written notice from Landlords of a disturbance violation, any repeated violation shall constitute a material breach of this Lease.

## 32. REMEDIES CUMULATIVE [NEW]

All rights and remedies of Landlords under this Lease are cumulative, not exclusive, and in addition to any rights or remedies available at law or in equity. The exercise by Landlords of any one or more rights or remedies shall not preclude the simultaneous or subsequent exercise of any other right or remedy. No delay or failure by Landlords to exercise any right or remedy shall constitute or be deemed a waiver of that right or remedy, or of any other right or remedy. A waiver of any breach shall not be deemed a waiver of any subsequent breach of the same or any other provision.

## 33. BINDING EFFECT; SUCCESSORS AND ASSIGNS [NEW]

This Lease shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of Landlords and Tenants and their respective heirs, legal representatives, successors, and permitted assigns. All covenants, conditions, and obligations of this Lease shall run with and bind the Premises during the Lease term. Any person to whom Tenants' interest is transferred — whether by assignment, sublease, operation of law, or otherwise and whether or not consented to by Landlords — shall be bound by all terms and conditions of this Lease, provided that such binding does not limit Landlords' right to treat any unauthorized transfer as a breach.
